You were seen once. Your next appointment is in six months. The flares don't wait that long.

Everything you need already exists — across Arthritis Australia, the NSW Pain Network, painHEALTH, the Pain Toolkit, Painaustralia and half a dozen others. None of it is findable at 2am with a joint on fire. Co-Motion is the front door: ask a question in your own words, get an answer from those sources, and get told what to do tonight.

Open Co-Motion → Free. Nothing to sign up for. Nothing about you is stored on our side.

Every answer ends somewhere

The point isn't to tell you things. It's to tell you what to do — and, most of the time, that it can safely be handled where you are. Every answer lands on one of five rungs.

  • You can do this at homeMost questions land here. That's the goal.
  • Your pharmacist can help todayNo appointment. Talking to them costs nothing.
  • This one's for your GP, and it can waitWritten down for you, with the date, so you walk in with it.
  • Call your GP todayOr healthdirect — a nurse answers, any hour.
  • Get seen tonightRare. But this is the one it must never get wrong.

Two things we enforce in code, not in a prompt

It can escalate. It can't soften.

The dangerous cases never reach the model

A hot, swollen joint with a fever is an infected joint until proven otherwise — and it looks exactly like the gout flare you've had thirty times before. A rule engine screens for that before the AI runs, and if it fires, the AI is never called at all. No latency, no rewording, no judgement. It works with the AI switched off entirely.

It answers, or it says it can't

It cannot invent health advice

Every claim comes from a card written from a published Australian source, and the AI may only pick from those cards and explain them. Anything it makes up is dropped by the server. When the sources don't cover your question, it says so and writes the question down for your doctor — rather than guessing at someone who has no one to ask for six months.
